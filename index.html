I'm a self-taught web developer. Besides writing code, I enjoy things like furthering my education.

A bit about me

Let's start with some basics: I'm someone who likes to laugh and meet people. I'm all about going to concerts with my friends and discovering new music. A lot of people know me as the guy who eats... and eats... and eats. I like hanging out at places where the locals go. Photography is a big hooby of mine and I enjoy taking trips where I can go explore with my camera. I promise I won't judge you for the way you pronounce 'GIF'. I'm someone who likes a challenge, especially when it involves work, fitness or food.

Moving on to what I do: I'm a front-end JavaScript developer working towards becoming a full-stack web developer. My journey of learning to code began at the start of 2017 when I chose to make programming a daily practice; I signed up for FreeCodeCamp's online bootcamp and there was no turning back. Diving into the programming pool to see if I'd sink or swim is still the scariest and best decision I've ever made. I find it exciting that I still have much to learn and am ready to contribute to a team that supports my eagerness to write great code.

Being a new developer, finding my "dream job" is a big goal, but certainly not the measure of my success. My ambition is to continue sharpening my skills and commit myself to my career & personal projects. In the long-term, I hope that as I gain experience I'll grow into other areas such as mobile development, blockchain, data science, and machine learning. Wherever my focus shifts, I aim to educate and drive myself towards expertise. If programming has taught me anything, it's that the only true failure is giving up!
